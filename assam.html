Bhaona

Bhaona is a traditional theatrical form of Assam, introduced by saint-scholar Srimanta Sankardev in the 16th century to convey religious messages and Vaishnavite philosophy. Performed mainly in satras and namghars, it combines storytelling, dance, music, and dialogue, creating a spiritual and cultural experience.

Origin

Introduced by Srimanta Sankardev in the 16th century, Bhaona aimed to teach moral and spiritual lessons through dramatic performance in Assamese.

Themes

Themes are mostly drawn from the Bhagavata, Ramayana, and Mahabharata—focused on devotion, dharma, and the triumph of good over evil.

Performance

Bhaona is performed at night using classical Assamese music, Sanskritized dialogue, rhythmic movement, and dramatic narration.

Instruments

Drums like khol, cymbals (taal), and flute are used in Bhaona to enhance devotional atmosphere and storytelling rhythm.

Costume

Elaborate traditional Assamese costumes, turbans, face paint, and ornaments represent mythological characters vividly on stage.

Significance

Bhaona is not just a theatre form but a religious celebration—preserving Assamese culture, language, and spiritual heritage for centuries.
