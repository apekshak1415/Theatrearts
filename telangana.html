Oggu Katha

Oggu Katha is a traditional folklore-based storytelling art form of Telangana, performed by the Oggu community. These stories are musical renditions of mythological tales, especially those of Lord Mallanna, Beerappa, and Yellamma. The performance is a rich blend of narration, dance, music, and dramatic expression, keeping alive the oral traditions of rural Telangana.

Origin

Oggu Katha originated among the Kuruma and Yadava communities of Telangana. It is performed as a devotional offering and storytelling form centered around folk deities like Mallanna.

Themes

The stories depict the divine legends of Lord Mallanna, Beerappa, and Yellamma, highlighting devotion, power, love, and sacrifice.

Performance

Performances include powerful vocal storytelling, energetic body language, dramatic expressions, and are often held during festivals and jatras.

Instruments

Traditional instruments like the dhol, jaggu, and kanjira are used to enhance the storytelling with rhythm and energy.

Costume

Oggu performers wear traditional dhotis, turbans, bead necklaces, and hold symbols representing the deity being narrated.

Cultural Impact

Oggu Katha preserves oral traditions and local history, strengthening cultural identity and rural community bonding in Telangana.
